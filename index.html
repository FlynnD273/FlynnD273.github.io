About Me

I'm a CS Major at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, and was previously a homeschooled part-time Running Start student. I have an avid interest in mechanical engineering and computer programming.

Competing in FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) has provided me with many great opportunities for teamwork, leadership, self-driven learning, and organization. It let me explore the world of mechanical engineering, and allowed me a peek into the complexities of integrate software with hardware. I have also been programming from a young age.

I started out with MIT's Scratch when I was about 9 years old, and haven't stopped programming since. I am now versed in programming in JavaScript, C#, Java, C, and more recently, Python. With C#, I am very familiar with writing applications in Windows Presentation Foundation, Xamarin.Forms, and Unity3D. With Java, I am familiar with graphics libraries such as Swing, as well as developing Android apps.

  • Work Experience

    I have built high-quality, intuitive, and powerful Windows Presentation Foundation applications for employers. The applications range from technical diagram editors to SQL database editors to file search utilities.

  • Personal Projects

    I make projects to increase my day-to-day productivity in C# and Python. I also make projects when I learn about a new topic and want to dive deeper.

  • Hobbies

    Aside from learning all I can about programming, I also enjoy learning about making building quadcopters, 3D modeling and animation, visual effects, and digital art.
